Discovering Optical Discs

Optical discs have been a cornerstone in digital storage, offering various formats with unique features. Let's explore the diversity of optical discs, including the iconic CD (Compact Disc), versatile DVD (Digital Versatile Disc), and high-definition Blu-ray. Each format boasts unique storage capacities and serves specific purposes in the digital landscape.

DVD (Digital Versatile Disc)

The DVD is a widely used optical disc format for video and data storage. It offers higher storage capacity compared to CDs and is commonly used for movies, software, and large data files.

Blu-ray

Blu-ray is a high-definition optical disc format, known for its superior video and audio quality. It has a larger storage capacity than DVDs, making it ideal for high-definition movies and data storage.
